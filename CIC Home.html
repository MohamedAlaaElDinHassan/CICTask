  • Existing educational programs require supervision and monitoring of its operations.
    Frequently, an institution in a remote location, such as Canada, delivers an educational program to its receivers in Egypt and the Middle East.
    This program can exist either as a direct delivery process through distance education, or indirect delivery through a third party educational organization which is present in the country of delivery.
    In all cases, the resources required for delivery of the program by the primary provider of the education is remotely located from the receiver of the education, who is located in Egypt and the Middle East.
    To facilitate the delivery of such educational programs, CanWell can provide highly qualified resources to the remote educational provider for supervision and coordination of delivery of its educational program in Egypt and the Middle East.
    From the remote educational provider’s perspective, outsourcing delivery and supervision of educational programs significantly reduces the high costs of devoting resources specifically for this purpose.
    From the receiver’s standpoint, the presence of highly qualified resources specifically designated for this purpose ensures the high quality of Education is maintained, which is what the receiver expects to receive.
    From CanWell’s perspective, CanWell’s ability to pool its resources to simultaneously supervise and coordinate more than one program enables it to reduce costs and achieve a reasonable profit margin.
